Terminology

Luthier's Glossary

Don't know your nut from your saddle? Your truss rod from your trust fund? We've got you.

Action
The height of the strings above the frets. Low action = easier to play; too low = fret buzz. The goal is finding the sweet spot.
Binding
A strip of material (plastic, wood, or purfling) inlaid along the edge of the body, neck, or headstock. Decorative and protective.
Bolt-On
A neck joint where the neck is bolted (or screwed) to the body. Used in Fender-style builds. Easier to replace than set or through-neck joints.
Coil Split
A mod that bypasses one coil of a humbucker, giving you a single-coil sound. Adds versatility; sound is thinner than a true single coil.
Fret Dress
The process of levelling, re-crowning, and polishing frets to eliminate buzzing and inconsistencies. One of the highest-value guitar tech jobs.
Humbucker
A dual-coil pickup that cancels electromagnetic hum. Warmer, louder, and thicker-sounding than single coils. Used in Gibson-style guitars.
Intonation
How accurately a guitar plays in tune up the neck. Set by adjusting saddle position so the 12th fret harmonic matches the fretted note.
Luthier
A craftsperson who builds and/or repairs stringed instruments. The word comes from the French word for lute. We're electric luthiers.
Nut
The slotted piece at the top of the neck (between headstock and fretboard) that guides and spaces the strings. Material affects open-string tone and tuning stability.
Quartersawn
Timber cut so the growth rings run roughly perpendicular to the face. More stable, resists warping, and shows beautiful medullary ray figure in species like Oak.
Relief
The slight forward bow in a guitar neck, controlled by the truss rod. A small amount of relief (0.1–0.3mm) is usually desirable for playability.
Scale Length
The vibrating string length from nut to saddle. Fender-style = 648mm (25.5"). Gibson-style = 628mm (24.75"). Affects tension, feel, and tone.
Truss Rod
A steel rod running inside the neck that counteracts string tension. Adjusting it controls neck relief. Turn carefully and never force it.
Tonewood
Any timber used in instrument construction chosen for its acoustic properties. In electric guitars, the effect is subtle but real — body resonance affects sustain and character.
Zero Fret
A fret installed at the nut position so open strings have the same tone as fretted notes. Used on some vintage and custom builds for consistent timbre.
